# Week 4: Data Analytics and Business Intelligence

## Overview

Week 4 bridges data visualization (Week 3) and case studies (Week 5) by focusing on data analytics methodologies, business intelligence tools, and how organizations use data to drive digital transformation decisions. This week prepares you to analyze real-world digital transformation case studies.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this week, you will be able to:

- [ ] Understand the data analytics lifecycle and methodologies
- [ ] Identify key business intelligence (BI) tools and platforms
- [ ] Analyze data to extract business insights
- [ ] Create data-driven recommendations for digital transformation
- [ ] Understand the role of data in strategic decision-making

## Topics Covered

### 1. Data Analytics Lifecycle

**Stages:**
1. **Data Collection**: Gathering data from various sources
2. **Data Preparation**: Cleaning, transforming, and organizing data
3. **Data Analysis**: Applying analytical techniques
4. **Data Visualization**: Presenting insights visually
5. **Decision Making**: Using insights to inform strategy

### 2. Types of Data Analytics

**Descriptive Analytics** - "What happened?"
- Historical data analysis
- Reporting and dashboards
- Example: Sales reports, website traffic statistics

**Diagnostic Analytics** - "Why did it happen?"
- Root cause analysis
- Data drilling and investigation
- Example: Why did sales drop in Q3?

**Predictive Analytics** - "What will happen?"
- Forecasting and modeling
- Machine learning predictions
- Example: Predicting customer churn

**Prescriptive Analytics** - "What should we do?"
- Optimization recommendations
- Actionable insights
- Example: Optimal pricing strategy recommendations

### 3. Business Intelligence Tools

**Popular BI Platforms:**
- **Tableau**: Advanced visualization and dashboards
- **Power BI**: Microsoft's integrated BI solution
- **Qlik Sense**: Associative data model
- **Looker**: Modern BI with data modeling
- **RapidMiner**: Data science and analytics platform

### 4. Data-Driven Decision Making

**Framework:**
1. Define business questions
2. Identify relevant data sources
3. Analyze data using appropriate methods
4. Visualize findings
5. Make recommendations
6. Monitor outcomes

### 5. KPIs and Metrics for Digital Transformation

**Key Performance Indicators:**
- Customer engagement metrics
- Operational efficiency metrics
- Revenue and growth metrics
- Technology adoption rates
- Digital maturity scores

## Practical Exercises

### Exercise 1: Analytics Type Identification
For each scenario, identify the type of analytics:
- Analyzing last month's sales performance
- Predicting next quarter's revenue
- Recommending the best marketing channel
- Investigating why website traffic decreased

### Exercise 2: KPI Selection
Select 5 KPIs for a digital transformation initiative focused on:
- Improving customer experience
- Reducing operational costs
- Increasing online sales
